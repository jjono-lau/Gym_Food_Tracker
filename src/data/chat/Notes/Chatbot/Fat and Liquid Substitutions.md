# Fat and Liquid Substitutions

## Summary
Fat and Liquid Substitutions lists practical swaps for oils, water, buttermilk-style liquids, and other cooking liquids used across the meal notes.

## Key Points
- [[Sesame Oil]] can often be replaced with another cooking fat in a technical sense, but the chatbot should note that flavor depth changes in South Indian dishes.
- [[Water]] is the neutral fallback for thinning batters, stews, and sauces, though it cannot fully replace flavor-rich liquids without changing the result.
- [[Low Fat Buttermilk]] and diluted [[Low Fat Curd]] can sometimes substitute for each other when the goal is tang and light body.
- [[Light Coconut Milk]] adds more body and flavor than water, so the chatbot should explain when the swap makes the dish thinner and less rich.
- For triglyceride-focused answers, the chatbot should prefer swaps that avoid unnecessary added fat and keep the meal practical.
