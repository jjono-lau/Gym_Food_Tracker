# Dairy Coconut and Sour Ingredient Substitutions

## Summary
Dairy Coconut and Sour Ingredient Substitutions lists practical swaps for curd, buttermilk, coconut, coconut milk, tamarind, and related flavor-balancing ingredients.

## Key Points
- [[Low Fat Curd]] can often be thinned into a buttermilk-style replacement, while [[Low Fat Buttermilk]] can sometimes stand in for curd when thickness is less important.
- [[Coconut]] and [[Light Coconut Milk]] can sometimes substitute for each other in chutney, stew, or curry-style recipes if the chatbot explains the texture change.
- [[Tamarind]] and [[Raw Mango]] can both add sourness in some savory dishes, but the flavor profile is not identical and the swap changes the dish character.
- Coconut-based swaps should be used more carefully when the user asks for triglyceride-focused food suggestions because total fat quality still matters.
- The chatbot should explain when souring ingredients are flexible and when they are part of the defining flavor of the dish.
