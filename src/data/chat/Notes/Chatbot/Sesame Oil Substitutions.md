# Sesame Oil Substitutions

## Summary
Sesame Oil Substitutions explains when sesame oil can be replaced for cooking function and when the flavor loss should be acknowledged.

## Key Points
- [[Sesame Oil]] can often be replaced technically by another cooking fat, but the chatbot should say the flavor profile becomes less distinct.
- The biggest change is usually aroma and finish rather than whether the meal cooks at all.
- In strongly South Indian flavor profiles, sesame oil can be part of the character of the dish, not just a neutral fat.
- For triglyceride-focused answers, the chatbot should prioritize practical moderation and cooking method rather than pretending oil choice alone solves the problem.
- If the recipe uses only a small amount of sesame oil, omission or a neutral replacement may still leave the dish workable.
