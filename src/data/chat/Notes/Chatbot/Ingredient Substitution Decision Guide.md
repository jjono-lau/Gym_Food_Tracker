# Ingredient Substitution Decision Guide

## Summary
Ingredient Substitution Decision Guide explains how to decide whether a missing ingredient can be swapped directly, swapped with adjustments, or should be left unchanged.

## Key Points
- First check the ingredient role: base carbohydrate, protein, vegetable, spice, souring agent, fat, or liquid.
- Easy swaps usually happen when the replacement does the same job, such as one rice for another rice or one leafy vegetable for another leafy vegetable.
- Moderate swaps are possible when the replacement changes cooking time, water absorption, or flavor intensity and the chatbot explains that clearly.
- Hard swaps should be flagged when the missing ingredient is structurally important, such as fermentation ingredients, thickening ingredients, or a defining flavor in a simple dish.
- The chatbot should give the swap, explain what changes, and say when the dish may taste or feel different.
