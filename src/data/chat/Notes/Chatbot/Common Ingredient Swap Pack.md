# Common Ingredient Swap Pack

## Summary
Common Ingredient Swap Pack defines the most useful everyday ingredient substitutions the chatbot should use when helping with recipe adaptation.

## Key Points
- Rice swaps: brown rice, red rice, foxtail millet, little millet, and quinoa can often replace more refined grain choices depending on texture and cooking style.
- Refined carbohydrate swaps: oats, millets, legumes, and lentil-based options are usually better than highly refined white flour products when the goal is steadier energy.
- Fat swaps: use smaller amounts of healthier fats and avoid relying on deep-fried preparation when the goal is triglyceride management.
- Protein swaps: beans, lentils, moong dal, chana dal, and other legumes can often replace lower-fiber carbohydrate-heavy sides.
- Dairy swaps: lower-fat curd or buttermilk may work where a lighter option is needed, but the chatbot should note when the swap changes texture or tang.
