# Grain and Starch Substitutions

## Summary
Grain and Starch Substitutions lists practical swaps for rice, millets, oats, quinoa, poha, and other starch bases used across the meal notes.

## Key Points
- [[Brown Rice]] can often be swapped with [[Red Rice]], [[Foxtail Millet]], [[Little Millet]], [[Barnyard Millet]], or [[Quinoa]] when the dish still works as a grain bowl or rice-style side.
- [[Poha]] can sometimes be swapped with [[Red Rice]] flakes if the goal is a similar flattened-rice texture, but the final dish may be chewier.
- [[Broken Wheat]] can often be swapped with [[Foxtail Millet]], [[Little Millet]], or [[Samai]] in upma- or pongal-style dishes with small water adjustments.
- [[Rolled Oats]] can replace part of a grain base in porridges, kanji, or savory soft dishes, but they should not be treated as a direct swap in every fermented batter.
- The chatbot should warn that grain swaps often change cooking time, water absorption, and softness even when the meal still works.
