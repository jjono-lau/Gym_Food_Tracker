# When Not To Swap Ingredients

## Summary
When Not To Swap Ingredients explains when a missing ingredient is too central to the recipe structure, fermentation, or defining flavor to replace casually.

## Key Points
- Ingredients that control fermentation, binding, or batter structure should not be swapped casually without saying the recipe may fail.
- Strong identity ingredients in simple dishes may be technically replaceable but can change the meal into something different.
- The chatbot should be careful with direct swaps in idli, dosa, appam, and other batter-driven recipes because grain-legume balance matters.
- If the user is missing several key ingredients at once, the better answer may be to suggest a different recipe rather than forcing many weak substitutions.
- The safest swap answer is honest: say whether the dish will still work, what will change, and when a different recipe is the better path.
