# PCOS Food Guidance

## Summary
PCOS Food Guidance focuses on eating patterns that support insulin sensitivity, weight management when needed, and steadier energy rather than a single rigid diet.

## Key Points
- A useful baseline is a minimally processed eating pattern built around vegetables, fruit, legumes, whole grains, lean protein, nuts, seeds, and other high-fiber foods.
- Lower-glycemic and higher-fiber foods are often more helpful than frequent refined carbohydrates and sugary foods because insulin resistance is common in PCOS.
- Foods with a high glycemic load, such as sugary desserts, white bread, and heavily refined starches, are often worth limiting.
- There is no single mandatory PCOS diet, so restrictive elimination should not be the default unless a food clearly causes problems or a clinician recommends it.
