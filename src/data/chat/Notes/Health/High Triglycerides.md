# High Triglycerides

## Summary
High Triglycerides means the level of triglycerides in the blood is elevated, which can increase cardiovascular risk and, at very high levels, raise the risk of pancreatitis.

## Key Points
- Triglycerides are a type of fat in the blood. In adults, fasting levels below 150 mg/dL are considered healthy, while 150 mg/dL or higher is elevated.
- High triglycerides often cause no symptoms, so they are usually found on blood tests rather than by how a person feels.
- Very high triglycerides, especially above 500 mg/dL, raise the risk of acute pancreatitis and need medical attention.
- Triglyceride levels can rise with low physical activity, excess body weight, diabetes, high alcohol intake, and diets high in sugar and unhealthy fats.
- Treatment usually starts with heart-healthy food choices, regular exercise, weight management when needed, and medical review for underlying causes or medication decisions.
