# Stair Climber for High Triglycerides Training

## Summary
This note explains how to use [[Stair Climber]] for high triglycerides training with a focus on regular aerobic work, steady progression, and heart-health support.

## Key Points
- Main context: [[Stair Climber]] sits inside [[Cardio Equipment Hub]] and should be used within the broader guidance in [[High Triglycerides Exercise Guidance]].
- Priority: Use this equipment to build regular weekly activity because consistency matters more than occasional very hard sessions.
- Priority: Cardio volume and steady resistance training both support triglyceride management when done repeatedly over time.
- Priority: Keep sessions controlled and sustainable so training becomes part of a long-term routine.
- Best use: treat [[Stair Climber]] as a steady cardio option for building weekly training minutes.
- Good pattern: use intervals only after you can already sustain moderate, repeatable sessions comfortably.
- Avoid: turning every session into maximal effort, because missed sessions reduce the long-term benefit.
