# PCOS

## Summary
PCOS is a hormonal and metabolic condition that can affect ovulation, menstrual cycles, androgen levels, fertility, and insulin sensitivity.

## Key Points
- PCOS often involves at least two of these features: irregular or absent ovulation, higher androgen levels or symptoms of androgen excess, and polycystic-appearing ovaries.
- Common effects can include irregular periods, acne, excess facial or body hair, difficulty getting pregnant, and trouble with insulin resistance.
- PCOS is also linked with higher risk of prediabetes, type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, sleep apnea, and other cardiometabolic problems.
- Lifestyle changes can help reduce symptoms and related health risks, especially regular physical activity, healthy eating, and weight management when clinically appropriate.
- PCOS treatment can also include medicines, so lifestyle support should sit alongside medical follow-up rather than replace it.
