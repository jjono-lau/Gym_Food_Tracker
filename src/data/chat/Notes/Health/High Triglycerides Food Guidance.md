# High Triglycerides Food Guidance

## Summary
High Triglycerides Food Guidance centers on a heart-healthy eating pattern that lowers excess sugar, lowers unhealthy fat intake, and supports better blood lipid control.

## Key Points
- A strong default pattern is vegetables, fruit, beans, lentils, oats, whole grains, nuts, fish, and lean proteins with less saturated fat and fewer heavily processed foods.
- Reducing sugary drinks, desserts, and refined carbohydrates is especially important because these can push triglycerides higher.
- Limiting saturated fat and avoiding trans fat supports better overall lipid control.
- Alcohol can raise triglycerides, and if triglycerides are very high a clinician may advise stopping alcohol completely.
- In this vault, meal planning, ingredient selection, and shopping structure are the main links for putting this advice into practice.
