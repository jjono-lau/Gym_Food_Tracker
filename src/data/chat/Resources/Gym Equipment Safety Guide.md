# Gym Equipment Safety Guide

## Summary
Gym Equipment Safety Guide gives general setup, loading, and movement rules for using free weights, cardio machines, and selectorized machines safely.

## Key Points
- Do: adjust the seat, pad, handle, or console before the working set so the machine fits your body and the movement starts in a controlled position.
- Do: begin with a light load or easy pace, practice the motion first, and increase resistance only when the technique stays stable.
- Do: keep the full movement controlled on both the working and return phases, especially on cable and stack machines.
- Do: stop the set if you feel sharp pain, dizziness, numbness, or a loss of control.
- Do not: copy another person's setup if their limb lengths, mobility, and training goal differ from yours.
- Do not: slam weight stacks, jump onto moving cardio equipment, or skip safeties on rack-based lifts.
