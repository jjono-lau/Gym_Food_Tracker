# Millet Pongal with Pepper Mushroom

## Summary
This dinner keeps the palate warm and savory while leaning on vegetables and lentils more than fat. Millet Pongal with Pepper Mushroom brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 120 g [[Little Millet]]
- 60 g [[Split Moong Dal]]
- 140 g [[Mushroom]], sliced or diced
- 10 g [[Ginger]]
- 1 tsp [[Black Pepper]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 620 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Dry-roast the moong dal over medium-low heat for 2 minutes.
2. Cook the grain, dal, salt, and water together until very soft: 4 whistles or 22 minutes on low heat after boiling.
3. Temper oil with cumin, pepper, ginger, and curry leaves on medium heat for 40 seconds and fold into the pongal.
4. Quickly saute or add the vegetable garnish as specified and serve hot.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 366 per serving.
- Protein: 15 g per serving.
- Carbs: 63 g per serving.
- Fat: 7 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Pongal keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat with extra hot water over low heat until creamy. Prepare side vegetables fresh for contrast.
