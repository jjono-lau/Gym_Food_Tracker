# Brown Rice Appam with Tomato Kurma

## Summary
Built for an easy night meal, the recipe focuses on soft textures, digestibility, and controlled portions of whole grains. Brown Rice Appam with Tomato Kurma brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 130 g [[Brown Rice]]
- 20 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 20 g [[Coconut]]
- 220 ml [[Light Coconut Milk]]
- 220 g [[Tomato]], medium dice
- 60 g [[Green Peas]]
- 60 g [[Onion]]
- 10 g [[Ginger]]
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 7 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 620 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Cook the appam one at a time in an appachatti, swirling the batter for lacy edges and a thicker center.
2. For the stew, soften onion, ginger, chilli, and curry leaves in oil over medium heat for 2 minutes.
3. Add the vegetable, peas, salt, and 180 ml water; cover and cook on medium-low heat for 7 minutes until almost tender.
4. Add coconut milk and simmer gently on low heat for 4 minutes. Serve the stew with hot appam.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 18 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 38 minutes.
- Calories: 491 per serving.
- Protein: 12 g per serving.
- Carbs: 73 g per serving.
- Fat: 17 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Stew keeps 2 days chilled; appam batter keeps 2 days after fermentation. Reheat the stew on low heat and cook appam fresh for best quality.
