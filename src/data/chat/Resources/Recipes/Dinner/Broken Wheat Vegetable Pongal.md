# Broken Wheat Vegetable Pongal

## Summary
A lighter evening recipe designed to feel comforting without becoming heavy or starch-dense. Broken Wheat Vegetable Pongal brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 120 g [[Broken Wheat]]
- 60 g [[Split Moong Dal]]
- 140 g [[Beans]], sliced or diced
- 10 g [[Ginger]]
- 1 tsp [[Black Pepper]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 620 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Dry-roast the moong dal over medium-low heat for 2 minutes.
2. Cook the grain, dal, salt, and water together until very soft: 4 whistles or 22 minutes on low heat after boiling.
3. Temper oil with cumin, pepper, ginger, and curry leaves on medium heat for 40 seconds and fold into the pongal.
4. Quickly saute or add the vegetable garnish as specified and serve hot.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 373 per serving.
- Protein: 16 g per serving.
- Carbs: 71 g per serving.
- Fat: 5 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Pongal keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat with extra hot water over low heat until creamy. Prepare side vegetables fresh for contrast.
