# Ragi Mudde with Keerai Saaru

## Summary
The dish uses South Indian evening-tiffin logic: quick-cooking grains, legumes, and aromatic tempering for depth without richness. Ragi Mudde with Keerai Saaru brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Ragi Flour]]
- 620 ml [[Water]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 180 g [[Spinach]], chopped
- 70 g [[Toor Dal]], washed
- 90 g [[Tomato]], chopped
- 10 g [[Garlic]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 0.8 tsp [[Black Pepper]]
- 6 ml [[Sesame Oil]]

## Steps
1. Bring 360 ml water with salt to a rolling boil over high heat. Rain in the flour while stirring briskly; lower to medium-low and beat for 5 to 6 minutes until thick and glossy.
2. With wet hands, shape the hot cooked dough into 4 compact balls once cool enough to handle.
3. For the saaru, simmer dal, vegetable, tomato, garlic, cumin, pepper, and 260 ml water for 10 minutes on medium heat until lightly broth-like.
4. Serve the mudde hot with the saaru poured alongside or over torn pieces.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 12 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 32 minutes.
- Calories: 452 per serving.
- Protein: 17 g per serving.
- Carbs: 86 g per serving.
- Fat: 5 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Mudde is best fresh but can be held 1 day chilled. Steam or microwave covered to reheat. Saaru keeps 2 days refrigerated and should be reheated separately so the mudde does not become waterlogged.
