# Pesarattu with Mint Chutney

## Summary
This dinner keeps the palate warm and savory while leaning on vegetables and lentils more than fat. Pesarattu with Mint Chutney brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 150 g [[Green Gram]]
- 50 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 90 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 60 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 10 g [[Ginger]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 420 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat a seasoned tawa on medium-high heat for 2 minutes.
2. Pour 120 ml batter and spread thinly. Scatter the vegetable and onion if using, then drizzle a little oil around the edge.
3. Cook 2 to 3 minutes on medium heat until the base is speckled brown and the top is dry.
4. Flip briefly if needed and serve immediately with mint chutney.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 12 minutes.
- Cook time: 15 minutes.
- Total time: 27 minutes.
- Calories: 415 per serving.
- Protein: 25 g per serving.
- Carbs: 70 g per serving.
- Fat: 5 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Batter holds 1 to 3 days depending on the base. Reheat cooked dosas only on a dry tawa for 30 to 45 seconds per side. Store chutney separately and bring it to room temperature before serving.
