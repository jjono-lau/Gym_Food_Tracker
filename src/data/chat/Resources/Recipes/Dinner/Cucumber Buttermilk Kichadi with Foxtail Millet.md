# Cucumber Buttermilk Kichadi with Foxtail Millet

## Summary
The dish uses South Indian evening-tiffin logic: quick-cooking grains, legumes, and aromatic tempering for depth without richness. Cucumber Buttermilk Kichadi with Foxtail Millet brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 120 g [[Foxtail Millet]]
- 120 g [[Cucumber]], small dice
- 40 g [[Split Moong Dal]]
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely chopped
- 0.8 tsp [[Black Pepper]], crushed
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 180 ml [[Low Fat Buttermilk]]
- 6 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 720 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Combine the grain, moong dal, vegetable, salt, and water in a pot and bring to a boil over high heat.
2. Reduce to low heat and cook covered for 18 to 20 minutes, stirring twice, until the mixture turns soft and porridge-like.
3. Heat oil in a small pan, add cumin, pepper, and ginger, and cook on medium heat for 30 seconds. Stir into the kanji.
4. Cool for 2 minutes, then fold in the buttermilk for a softer finish before serving.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 22 minutes.
- Total time: 32 minutes.
- Calories: 356 per serving.
- Protein: 16 g per serving.
- Carbs: 58 g per serving.
- Fat: 7 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Kanji keeps 2 days chilled and thickens substantially. Reheat with extra hot water over low heat, then adjust seasoning. For batch cooking, add buttermilk only to the portion being served.
