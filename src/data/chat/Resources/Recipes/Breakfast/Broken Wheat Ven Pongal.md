# Broken Wheat Ven Pongal

## Summary
The recipe uses gentle seasoning and controlled oil so the grain, lentil, and vegetable flavors stay clear and clean. Broken Wheat Ven Pongal brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 120 g [[Broken Wheat]]
- 60 g [[Split Moong Dal]]
- 100 g [[Bottle Gourd]], small dice
- 12 g [[Ginger]], julienned
- 1 tsp [[Black Pepper]], crushed
- 1.2 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 600 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Dry-roast the moong dal over medium-low heat for 2 minutes until it smells nutty but does not color.
2. Combine the grain, dal, vegetable, salt, and water in a pressure cooker or heavy pot. Cook until very soft: 4 whistles in a cooker or 22 to 25 minutes covered on low heat after boiling.
3. In a small pan, heat oil on medium heat, add cumin, pepper, ginger, and curry leaves, and cook for 40 seconds until fragrant.
4. Fold the tempering into the cooked pongal and beat vigorously with a ladle for 1 minute so the starch loosens into a creamy spoonable texture.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 359 per serving.
- Protein: 15 g per serving.
- Carbs: 68 g per serving.
- Fat: 5 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Pongal keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat with 60 to 80 ml hot water in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until creamy again. For batch cooking, hold the tempering separately and fold it in after reheating for a fresher spice profile.
