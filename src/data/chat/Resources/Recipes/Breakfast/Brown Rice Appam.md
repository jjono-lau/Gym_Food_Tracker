# Brown Rice Appam

## Summary
The recipe uses gentle seasoning and controlled oil so the grain, lentil, and vegetable flavors stay clear and clean. Brown Rice Appam brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 120 g [[Brown Rice]], washed
- 20 g [[Urad Dal]], washed
- 25 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 120 ml [[Light Coconut Milk]]
- 80 g [[Green Peas]], small dice
- 0.5 tsp [[Fenugreek Seeds]]
- 6 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 320 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat an appachatti over medium heat and grease it with a few drops of oil.
2. Pour 120 ml batter into the center, lift the pan immediately, and swirl so the batter coats the sides thinly while a thicker pool remains in the middle.
3. Scatter the vegetable into the center only, cover, and cook on medium-low heat for 2.5 to 3 minutes until the edges lace and the middle looks set but springy.
4. Do not flip. Remove with a flat spatula and serve hot with tomato chutney.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 15 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 407 per serving.
- Protein: 10 g per serving.
- Carbs: 61 g per serving.
- Fat: 13 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Appam batter keeps 2 days chilled after fermentation. Cooked appam is best the same day, but it can be reheated in a covered pan for 1 minute over low heat. Ferment the batter overnight and cook fresh for the best edge texture.
