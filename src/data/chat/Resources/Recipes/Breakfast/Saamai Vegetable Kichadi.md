# Saamai Vegetable Kichadi

## Summary
This morning dish leans on traditional South Indian technique but shifts the grain balance toward whole grains and lentils. Saamai Vegetable Kichadi brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 130 g [[Samai]]
- 45 g [[Split Moong Dal]]
- 140 g [[Carrot]], small dice
- 60 g [[Onion]], small dice
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely chopped
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 520 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat oil in a cooker or heavy pot on medium heat. Add mustard, cumin, and curry leaves; once aromatic, add onion, ginger, chilli, and vegetables and cook 3 minutes.
2. Add the grain, dal, salt, and water and bring to a boil over high heat.
3. Pressure cook for 3 whistles or cover and cook on low heat for 18 to 20 minutes until the grain is soft and spoonable.
4. Rest 5 minutes, then stir briskly once to unify the starch and vegetables before serving.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 382 per serving.
- Protein: 13 g per serving.
- Carbs: 69 g per serving.
- Fat: 8 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Keeps 2 days refrigerated. Reheat with 3 to 4 tbsp hot water over low heat. For batch cooking, undercook by 1 minute if you know the dish will be reheated later.
