# Ulundhu Kali with Buttermilk

## Summary
This morning dish leans on traditional South Indian technique but shifts the grain balance toward whole grains and lentils. Ulundhu Kali with Buttermilk brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 140 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 220 ml [[Low Fat Buttermilk]]
- 320 ml [[Water]]
- 1 tsp [[Salt]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 8 g [[Ginger]], grated

## Steps
1. Bring 200 ml water with salt, cumin, and ginger to a boil over medium heat.
2. Whisk in the slurry steadily and lower the heat. Cook for 6 to 8 minutes, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and leaves the sides of the pan.
3. Switch to low heat and beat in the warm buttermilk a little at a time until smooth and glossy.
4. Cook 1 final minute, then serve soft with a spoon rather than letting it set fully firm.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 8 minutes.
- Cook time: 12 minutes.
- Total time: 20 minutes.
- Calories: 286 per serving.
- Protein: 21 g per serving.
- Carbs: 47 g per serving.
- Fat: 2 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Kali thickens as it cools. Refrigerate up to 2 days and reheat over low heat with extra hot water or buttermilk to loosen. For make-ahead service, stop cooking when slightly looser than desired; it firms on standing.
