# Red Aval Vegetable Upma

## Summary
The recipe uses gentle seasoning and controlled oil so the grain, lentil, and vegetable flavors stay clear and clean. Red Aval Vegetable Upma brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 150 g [[Poha]]
- 120 g [[Capsicum]], small dice
- 70 g [[Onion]], small dice
- 60 g [[Carrot]], small dice
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely chopped
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], finely chopped
- 18 g [[Chana Dal]]
- 12 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 420 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Set a kadai over medium heat and dry-roast the main grain for 3 to 4 minutes until nutty and no longer smells raw. Remove to a plate.
2. Add oil, mustard seeds, chana dal, and urad dal; cook on medium heat for 1 minute until the dals turn pale golden. Add curry leaves, ginger, chilli, onion, and vegetables and saute for 3 minutes until the onion turns translucent.
3. Pour in water, salt, and bring to a brisk boil over high heat. Rain in the roasted grain while stirring steadily to avoid lumps.
4. Reduce to low heat, cover, and cook 6 to 8 minutes until the liquid is absorbed and the grains are tender but separate. Rest 3 minutes before fluffing.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 12 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 402 per serving.
- Protein: 10 g per serving.
- Carbs: 78 g per serving.
- Fat: 6 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Upma keeps 2 days refrigerated. Reheat in a covered pan with 2 tbsp water over low heat for 3 minutes to restore softness. For batch cooking, roast the grain and portion the tempering ingredients ahead so breakfast comes together quickly.
