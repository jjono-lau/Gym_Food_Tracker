# Moringa Leaf Dosa

## Summary
Built for a slower glucose rise, this breakfast keeps the familiar hotel-style texture without relying on refined starch. Moringa Leaf Dosa brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 140 g [[Brown Rice]], washed
- 50 g [[Urad Dal]], washed
- 60 g [[Brown Rice]]
- 1 tsp [[Fenugreek Seeds]]
- 60 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 80 g [[Drumstick Leaves]], finely chopped
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely grated
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], finely chopped
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]], thinly sliced
- 10 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 420 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat a cast-iron dosa tawa over medium heat for 3 minutes; sprinkle a few drops of water and wipe when it sizzles and vanishes immediately.
2. Pour 120 ml batter in the center, spread outward in a thin spiral, and drizzle 2 ml oil around the edges. Cook on medium heat for 2 to 3 minutes until the surface dries and the base turns deep golden.
3. Flip only if the topping looks moist; cook the second side on low heat for 45 to 60 seconds until set but not crisp.
4. Repeat with the remaining batter, adjusting the heat so each dosa browns gradually rather than blistering. Serve hot with tomato chutney.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 33 minutes.
- Calories: 543 per serving.
- Protein: 18 g per serving.
- Carbs: 99 g per serving.
- Fat: 9 g per serving.
- Meal prep: The batter keeps 3 days in the fridge; stir gently before reuse. Cooked dosas reheat best on a dry pan over low heat for 1 minute per side. For batch cooking, ferment the batter in one container and portion toppings separately so later rounds stay crisp.
