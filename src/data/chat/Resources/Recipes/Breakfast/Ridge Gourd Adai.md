# Ridge Gourd Adai

## Summary
A fermented tiffin that stays light on the stomach while delivering enough fiber to keep breakfast steady. Ridge Gourd Adai brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 70 g [[Chana Dal]], washed
- 45 g [[Chana Dal]], washed
- 35 g [[Toor Dal]], washed
- 20 g [[Urad Dal]], washed
- 100 g [[Ridge Gourd]], very finely chopped
- 70 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 12 g [[Ginger]], grated
- 4 tsp [[Dry Red Chilli]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 5 g [[Curry Leaves]], sliced
- 10 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 320 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat the tawa over medium heat. Spread 130 ml batter into a medium-thick round, keeping small gaps for steam to escape.
2. Drizzle 2 ml oil around the perimeter and cook on medium heat for 3 minutes until the edges look dry and the underside turns chestnut brown.
3. Flip and cook on medium-low heat for 2 more minutes until the center feels firm and the chopped vegetables no longer look raw.
4. Serve hot with tomato chutney, keeping the adai thicker than dosa so the coarse lentils stay juicy rather than brittle.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 33 minutes.
- Calories: 379 per serving.
- Protein: 19 g per serving.
- Carbs: 58 g per serving.
- Fat: 9 g per serving.
- Meal prep: The batter keeps 2 days in the fridge and thickens slightly; loosen with 1 to 2 tbsp water before cooking again. Cooked adai reheat well in an airless skillet over low heat for 90 seconds per side. Batch-cook and cool fully before stacking between parchment.
