# Keerai Pesarattu

## Summary
Built for a slower glucose rise, this breakfast keeps the familiar hotel-style texture without relying on refined starch. Keerai Pesarattu brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 150 g [[Green Gram]], washed
- 80 g [[Spinach]], finely chopped
- 70 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 10 g [[Ginger]]
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], finely chopped
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]], shredded
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 260 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat the tawa on medium-high heat and wipe it with a thin film of oil.
2. Pour 120 ml batter and spread it quickly into a thin round before the surface sets. Scatter the onion mixture evenly and press lightly with the back of the ladle.
3. Cook on medium heat for 2.5 to 3 minutes until the edges lift easily and the base turns mottled brown. Drizzle 2 ml oil along the edge.
4. Flip for 45 seconds only if needed; the second side should stay soft. Serve immediately with tomato chutney.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 10 minutes.
- Cook time: 15 minutes.
- Total time: 25 minutes.
- Calories: 325 per serving.
- Protein: 20 g per serving.
- Carbs: 53 g per serving.
- Fat: 5 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Batter holds 24 hours refrigerated; beyond that it loses the fresh green gram flavor. Reheat cooked pesarattu on a dry skillet for 45 seconds per side. For batch prep, soak and refrigerate the drained beans a day ahead, then grind fresh.
