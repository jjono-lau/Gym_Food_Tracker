# Methi Ragi Uthappam

## Summary
Built for a slower glucose rise, this breakfast keeps the familiar hotel-style texture without relying on refined starch. Methi Ragi Uthappam brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 130 g [[Brown Rice]], washed
- 55 g [[Urad Dal]], washed
- 50 g [[Ragi Flour]]
- 1 tsp [[Fenugreek Seeds]]
- 80 g [[Onion]], finely chopped
- 90 g [[Methi Leaves]], small dice
- 70 g [[Tomato]], small dice
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]], chopped
- 0.5 tsp [[Black Pepper]]
- 10 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 430 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Warm the tawa on medium heat for 2 minutes and grease it lightly with 1 ml oil.
2. Pour 140 ml batter without spreading it too thin; nudge it only enough to form a 12 to 14 cm round. Scatter one-quarter of the topping mix over it and press gently.
3. Drizzle 2 ml oil around the edge and cook on medium-low heat for 3 minutes until the sides look dry and the underside turns evenly golden.
4. Flip carefully and cook on low heat for 1 to 1.5 minutes until the vegetables soften and faint brown patches appear. Serve immediately.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 35 minutes.
- Calories: 509 per serving.
- Protein: 17 g per serving.
- Carbs: 92 g per serving.
- Fat: 8 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Fermented batter holds for 3 days refrigerated. Reheat cooked uthappam covered on a skillet over low heat for 2 minutes to restore softness. Chop toppings fresh; pre-mixed salted vegetables will leak water overnight.
