# Thinai Coconut Puttu

## Summary
Built for a slower glucose rise, this breakfast keeps the familiar hotel-style texture without relying on refined starch. Thinai Coconut Puttu brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Thinai]]
- 25 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 60 g [[Green Gram]], cooked until tender
- 1 tsp [[Salt]]
- 110 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Fill the puttu cylinder in alternating layers of grated coconut, damp flour, and cooked green gram, finishing with a spoon of coconut.
2. Steam over medium heat for 6 to 8 minutes until a strong column of steam escapes from the top and the puttu smells fully cooked.
3. Rest 1 minute, then push out gently onto a plate so the cylinder stays intact.
4. Serve warm, breaking it up lightly with a fork before eating.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 10 minutes.
- Total time: 25 minutes.
- Calories: 429 per serving.
- Protein: 17 g per serving.
- Carbs: 71 g per serving.
- Fat: 8 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Puttu is best fresh but can be refrigerated for 1 day. Reheat by steaming for 3 minutes. For batch prep, pre-moisten the flour and hold it refrigerated in an airtight box, then steam portions to order.
