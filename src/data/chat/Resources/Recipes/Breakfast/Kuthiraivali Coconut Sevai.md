# Kuthiraivali Coconut Sevai

## Summary
This morning dish leans on traditional South Indian technique but shifts the grain balance toward whole grains and lentils. Kuthiraivali Coconut Sevai brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Kuthiraivali]]
- 90 g [[Cabbage]], julienned
- 60 g [[Onion]], thinly sliced
- 20 g [[Coconut]], freshly grated
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely chopped
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], finely chopped
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 12 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 320 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat a wide pan on medium heat, add oil, mustard seeds, urad dal, and cook 45 seconds until the dal turns pale gold.
2. Add curry leaves, ginger, chilli, onion, and vegetables; saute 3 to 4 minutes until just tender but still bright.
3. Add the sevai, salt, and 40 ml water. Toss gently on low heat for 2 minutes so the strands absorb seasoning without breaking.
4. Finish with grated coconut and toss once more. Rest 2 minutes before serving so the starch firms and the strands stay separate.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 12 minutes.
- Cook time: 12 minutes.
- Total time: 24 minutes.
- Calories: 366 per serving.
- Protein: 12 g per serving.
- Carbs: 56 g per serving.
- Fat: 11 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Cooked sevai keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat in a covered skillet over low heat with 1 tbsp water for 2 minutes. For batch prep, steam or cook the sevai base ahead and season it only just before eating.
