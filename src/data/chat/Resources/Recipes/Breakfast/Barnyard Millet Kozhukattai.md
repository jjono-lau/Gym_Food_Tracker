# Barnyard Millet Kozhukattai

## Summary
Built for a slower glucose rise, this breakfast keeps the familiar hotel-style texture without relying on refined starch. Barnyard Millet Kozhukattai brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 150 g [[Barnyard Millet]]
- 100 g [[Carrot]], finely chopped
- 18 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], finely chopped
- 10 g [[Ginger]], finely chopped
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 12 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 340 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat oil in a saute pan on medium heat. Add mustard seeds and urad dal and cook until the dal is lightly golden. Stir in curry leaves, ginger, chilli, and the vegetable; cook 2 minutes.
2. Pour in water and salt and bring to a full boil over high heat. Lower the heat and stir in the grain slowly until it forms a soft mass.
3. Cover and rest off heat for 5 minutes. When cool enough to handle, shape into small oblong dumplings with damp hands.
4. Steam on medium heat for 10 to 12 minutes until the surface looks matte and firm. Serve warm with chutney or a thin lentil broth.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 14 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 32 minutes.
- Calories: 345 per serving.
- Protein: 11 g per serving.
- Carbs: 52 g per serving.
- Fat: 10 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Kozhukattai keeps 3 days refrigerated. Reheat by steaming for 4 minutes, not in a dry pan, so the interior stays soft. For batch cooking, shape and refrigerate on a tray, then steam only what you need.
