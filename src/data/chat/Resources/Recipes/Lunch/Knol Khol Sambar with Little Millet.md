# Knol Khol Sambar with Little Millet

## Summary
Traditional tempering and slow simmering deepen the flavor so the meal tastes complete even with minimal oil. Knol Khol Sambar with Little Millet brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Little Millet]], washed
- 90 g [[Toor Dal]], washed
- 200 g [[Knol Khol]], medium chunks
- 70 g [[Onion]], sliced
- 100 g [[Tomato]], chopped
- 12 g [[Tamarind]]
- 2 tbsp [[Sambar Powder]]
- 0.5 tsp [[Turmeric]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.5 tsp [[Salt]]
- 950 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat oil in a pot on medium heat. Add mustard and curry leaves; once crackling, add onion and cook 3 minutes until translucent.
2. Add tomato, turmeric, sambar powder, and the vegetable; cook 3 minutes until the tomato softens and the spice loses its raw edge.
3. Pour in tamarind water and 450 ml fresh water. Simmer on medium heat for 10 minutes until the vegetable is tender but intact.
4. Whisk in the cooked dal and simmer another 6 minutes on low heat until the broth thickens slightly and coats a spoon. Serve over the cooked grain.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 18 minutes.
- Cook time: 28 minutes.
- Total time: 46 minutes.
- Calories: 515 per serving.
- Protein: 20 g per serving.
- Carbs: 94 g per serving.
- Fat: 9 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Sambar and cooked grain keep 3 days chilled. Reheat the sambar over medium-low heat for 5 minutes, thinning with hot water if it has thickened. Batch-cook the dal and grain separately so lunch portions can be assembled without overcooking the vegetables.
