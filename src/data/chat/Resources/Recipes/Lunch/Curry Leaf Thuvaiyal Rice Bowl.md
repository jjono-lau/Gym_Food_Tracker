# Curry Leaf Thuvaiyal Rice Bowl

## Summary
The dish is layered like a proper home-style lunch, with acidity, spice, and texture tuned for a satisfying but lighter plate. Curry Leaf Thuvaiyal Rice Bowl brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Brown Rice]], washed
- 140 g [[Curry Leaves]], chopped
- 45 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 15 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 8 g [[Tamarind]]
- 3 tsp [[Dry Red Chilli]]
- 12 g [[Urad Dal]]
- 7 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 520 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Roast the pulse accent and chillies in a small pan on medium-low heat until fragrant. Add the vegetable if it benefits from wilting or softening and cook until concentrated.
2. Grind with tamarind, coconut, salt, and a little water into a thick paste.
3. Warm the paste briefly in the pan with 1 tsp oil if you want a mellower flavor, then toss through the cooked grain or serve it in a thick layer over the grain bowl.
4. Let the bowl stand 2 minutes before eating so the paste seasons the grain evenly.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 14 minutes.
- Cook time: 16 minutes.
- Total time: 30 minutes.
- Calories: 526 per serving.
- Protein: 18 g per serving.
- Carbs: 93 g per serving.
- Fat: 9 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Thuvaiyal keeps 4 days chilled. Store separately from the grain and combine only at serving time. It also freezes well in small portions for quick lunches.
