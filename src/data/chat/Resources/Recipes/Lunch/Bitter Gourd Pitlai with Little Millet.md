# Bitter Gourd Pitlai with Little Millet

## Summary
Traditional tempering and slow simmering deepen the flavor so the meal tastes complete even with minimal oil. Bitter Gourd Pitlai with Little Millet brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Little Millet]], washed
- 70 g [[Black Chana]], cooked tender
- 220 g [[Okra]], medium chunks
- 45 g [[Toor Dal]], washed
- 20 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 1.5 tsp [[Coriander Seeds]]
- 12 g [[Chana Dal]]
- 3 tsp [[Dry Red Chilli]]
- 10 g [[Tamarind]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 8 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.5 tsp [[Salt]]
- 920 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Simmer the vegetable in tamarind water and 300 ml fresh water over medium heat for 10 minutes until tender.
2. Add the cooked toor dal, cooked pulse accent, ground spice paste, and salt. Cook on low heat for 8 minutes until the gravy thickens and the raw roast aroma disappears.
3. Temper mustard and curry leaves in oil and pour into the pot.
4. Rest 5 minutes before serving over the grain.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 20 minutes.
- Cook time: 24 minutes.
- Total time: 44 minutes.
- Calories: 608 per serving.
- Protein: 24 g per serving.
- Carbs: 103 g per serving.
- Fat: 14 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Pitlai keeps 3 days chilled and reheats well over low heat. It also freezes well for 1 month. Store the cooked grain separately to avoid an over-thick meal box.
