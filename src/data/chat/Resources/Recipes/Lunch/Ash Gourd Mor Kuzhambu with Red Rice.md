# Ash Gourd Mor Kuzhambu with Red Rice

## Summary
A balanced South Indian lunch bowl where legumes, vegetables, and whole grains carry the dish instead of excess fat. Ash Gourd Mor Kuzhambu with Red Rice brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Red Rice]], washed
- 220 g [[Ash Gourd]], medium chunks
- 220 g [[Low Fat Curd]]
- 20 g [[Coconut]], grated
- 15 g [[Toor Dal]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]]
- 0.5 tsp [[Turmeric]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 0.2 tsp [[Fenugreek Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 7 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 700 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Simmer the vegetable in 250 ml water on medium heat for 6 to 8 minutes until barely tender.
2. Add the ground paste and cook on low heat for 4 minutes, stirring often so the paste loses its raw edge.
3. Lower the heat fully and pour in the whisked curd mixture. Heat gently for 3 to 4 minutes, stirring constantly, until steam rises and tiny bubbles appear at the edge; do not boil.
4. Temper mustard, fenugreek, and curry leaves in oil and pour over the mor kuzhambu. Serve immediately with the cooked grain.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 18 minutes.
- Cook time: 20 minutes.
- Total time: 38 minutes.
- Calories: 463 per serving.
- Protein: 12 g per serving.
- Carbs: 76 g per serving.
- Fat: 11 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Best eaten fresh, though it keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat only on low heat, stirring constantly, until just warm. For meal prep, cook the vegetable and paste base ahead and add the curd only when serving.
