# Chayote Coconut Stew with Red Rice

## Summary
A balanced South Indian lunch bowl where legumes, vegetables, and whole grains carry the dish instead of excess fat. Chayote Coconut Stew with Red Rice brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Red Rice]], washed
- 240 g [[Chayote]], medium cubes
- 70 g [[Cowpea]], cooked tender
- 60 g [[Onion]], sliced
- 220 ml [[Light Coconut Milk]]
- 10 g [[Ginger]], julienned
- 2 tsp [[Green Chilli]], slit
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 6 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 680 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Heat oil in a pot on medium heat and soften onion, ginger, chilli, and curry leaves for 3 minutes without browning.
2. Add the vegetable and 250 ml water, cover, and cook on medium-low heat for 8 minutes until almost tender.
3. Add the cooked pulse accent, coconut milk, and salt and simmer gently on low heat for 5 minutes until the broth lightly coats the spoon.
4. Serve the stew over or beside the cooked grain.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 33 minutes.
- Calories: 561 per serving.
- Protein: 17 g per serving.
- Carbs: 95 g per serving.
- Fat: 13 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Stew keeps 2 days chilled. Reheat only on low heat so the coconut milk stays smooth. Batch-cook the grain and pulse separately; finish the coconut-milk phase shortly before serving.
