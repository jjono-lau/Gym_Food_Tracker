# Curry Leaf Pepper Rasam with Red Rice

## Summary
Traditional tempering and slow simmering deepen the flavor so the meal tastes complete even with minimal oil. Curry Leaf Pepper Rasam with Red Rice brings a distinct structure, seasoning pattern, and grain-legume balance to the table.

## Ingredients
- 160 g [[Red Rice]], washed
- 50 g [[Toor Dal]], cooked soft
- 120 g [[Curry Leaves]], chopped or crushed
- 100 g [[Tomato]], chopped
- 10 g [[Tamarind]]
- 0.8 tsp [[Black Pepper]]
- 1 tsp [[Cumin Seeds]]
- 12 g [[Garlic]]
- 1 tsp [[Mustard Seeds]]
- 4 g [[Curry Leaves]]
- 6 ml [[Sesame Oil]]
- 1.2 tsp [[Salt]]
- 900 ml [[Water]]

## Steps
1. Combine tamarind water, tomato, vegetable element, salt, and 500 ml water in a pot and simmer on medium heat for 8 minutes.
2. Add the cooked pulse and the crushed pepper-cumin-garlic mixture and simmer 4 minutes more until the raw garlic smell disappears.
3. Temper mustard and curry leaves in oil over medium heat and pour over the rasam. Switch off the heat as soon as the rasam foams lightly at the edges.
4. Rest 2 minutes, then ladle over the cooked grain.

## Nutrition Notes
- Serves 2.
- Prep time: 15 minutes.
- Cook time: 18 minutes.
- Total time: 33 minutes.
- Calories: 485 per serving.
- Protein: 16 g per serving.
- Carbs: 92 g per serving.
- Fat: 6 g per serving.
- Meal prep: Rasam keeps 2 days refrigerated. Reheat only until steaming, not boiling. Batch-cook the grain separately and make the rasam fresh if possible because its top-note aromas fade faster than heavier gravies.
