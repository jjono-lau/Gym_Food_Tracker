Triglycerides

Heart-health basics and practical food direction

💧

What are high triglycerides

Triglycerides are a type of fat in the blood, and higher levels can raise cardiovascular risk. Very high triglycerides can also raise the risk of pancreatitis.

Aim for steady energy, not sugar spikes.

🥗

What should I eat for high triglycerides

For high triglycerides, emphasize vegetables, legumes, whole grains, lean proteins, less added sugar, and less heavily processed food.

Think veggies, legumes, whole grains, and lean protein first.

🏃‍♀️

What exercise helps high triglycerides

For high triglycerides, regular aerobic activity supported by resistance training usually works better than occasional maximal sessions.

Repeatable cardio plus strength beats random all-out sessions.

⛔

What should I avoid with high triglycerides?

Sugary drinks, desserts, refined carbohydrates, trans fat, excess saturated fat, and alcohol are the highest-yield things to cut back because they can push triglycerides higher.

Reduce sugar first, then watch alcohol and heavily processed foods.

PCOS

Steadier energy, meal balance, and training consistency

🌿

What is PCOS

PCOS is a hormonal and metabolic condition that can affect periods, ovulation, androgen levels, and insulin sensitivity.

Think hormones, insulin sensitivity, and steady routines instead of one perfect fix.

✨

What should I eat for PCOS

For PCOS, aim for minimally processed foods, higher-fiber carbohydrates, protein support, and balanced meals instead of chasing one perfect diet.

Balanced meals and consistency matter more than a perfect diet.

🏋️‍♀️

What exercise helps PCOS

The best exercise approach for PCOS is usually regular aerobic exercise plus strength training, with consistency and recovery taking priority over extreme intensity.

Regular cardio plus strength training usually works better than extreme intensity.

🚫

What should I avoid with PCOS?

Sugary desserts, white bread, heavily refined starches, and frequent high-glycemic foods are often worth limiting because they can make energy and insulin control harder.

Start by cutting back the sugary and heavily refined foods you eat most often.
